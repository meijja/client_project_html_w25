Health and Wellness Resources

The University of Michigan School of Information (UMSI) takes all aspects of student well-being seriously. By offering a variety of counseling and health services, we want our students to thrive academically, emotionally, and socially during their time at the university. UMSI also fosters a supportive community through peer mentorship programs and stress-reduction initiatives, ensuring that every student feels valued and empowered to reach their full potential. We aim to create an environment where students can excel in their studies while maintaining a healthy and balanced lifestyle.

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS)

CAPS is committed to allowing the university's diverse student body access to care and high quality services in order to take positive pathways to mental health. This includes:

  • Uwill Teletherapy Services: Provides students with access to a therapist of their choice via the mode of their choice - video, phone, chat or messaging.
  • Individual Peer Counseling: Trained undergraduate and graduate students at UM who can listen and relate, and provide resources where needed.
  • Togetherall: An online, anonymous peer-support tool that is clinically moderated to seek and provide support.

Basic Needs

U-M believes in fair and equitable access to food and other basic resources. The Basic Needs website contains resources on navigating finances, food, health care, housing, and transportion. This includes resources such as:

  • Maize and Blue Cupboard: Provides food, kitchen and cleaning supplies, as well as household supplies--appointment-only.
  • University Health Service (UHS): Provides students with affordable, on-campus health care options, including a variety of services that result in no out-of-pocket cost.
  • Careers at U-M: Provides a variety of employment opportunities, including professional, temporary and student jobs..
